Policy Effectiveness Analysis Flow

Quantitatively analyze the effectiveness of platform policy changes (Bans, Content Moderation Updates) on hate speech prevalence on social media platforms.

I. Learnings from Reading & Discussion

A. The "Displacement Effect"

Insight: Banning toxicity merely shifts the problem. Users migrate to less-moderated platforms or create new accounts, meaning platform-specific bans do not solve the root issue.

Source: Reddit 2015 Ban (R/FPH, R/CoonTown)

B. Policy Impact Measurement is Complex

Insight: Success should not be measured purely by general post counts. Tracking the fate of banned users (attrition of bad actors) is crucial for assessing true behavioral change.

Source: Reddit Analysis Methodology

C. Limitations of Text Analysis

Insight: Simple keyword-based tools (lexicons) fail to capture implicit hate speech, sarcasm, or "dog-whistles," severely underestimating the real volume of toxic content.

Source: Use of Lexicons and Keywords

D. Policy Rationale Varies by Platform

Insight: The business model (ad-driven vs. user-driven) and corporate priorities fundamentally shape the nature, scope, and enforcement of moderation policies.

Source: Discussion on Reddit vs. Instagram/X

II. Application to Project Plan (Actionable Steps)

1. Focus on User Attrition & Behavioral Changes

Methodology Track the percentage of targeted accounts that become permanently inactive or deleted following a ban, rather than relying only on post volume reduction.

2. Target Policy Comparison (Reddit vs. X/Twitter)

Data Set Select two platforms with divergent business models (e.g., Reddit and X/Twitter) to compare how similar policies yield different results based on their respective core structures.

3. Utilize Advanced Textual Metrics

Quantification Incorporate contextual analysis or sophisticated NLP models to move beyond simple word-matching and effectively detect implicit, sarcastic, or nuanced forms of hate speech.

4. Establish Concrete "Before/After" Timeline

Analysis Select a specific, dated policy change. Collect and compare data for equal pre-period (e.g., 6 months) and post-period timeframes to strengthen causal inference and properly analyze shifts in hate speech levels.

III. Clarity of Ideas

The project plan is directly informed by the key discussion takeaways, ensuring the methodology is robust and addresses the documented limitations of current policy effectiveness measurement.
